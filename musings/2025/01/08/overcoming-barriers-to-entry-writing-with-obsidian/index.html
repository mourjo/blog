In the 1998 paper ‚ÄúThe extended mind‚Äù, Clark and Chalmers posit that our human minds go beyond the boundaries of skin and skull ‚Äì that elements in our environment play a great role in driving our cognitive processes.

Where does the mind stop and the rest of the world begin? All the components in the system play an active causal role, and they jointly govern behaviour in the same sort of way that cognition usually does.

The authors call this active externalism ‚Äì an extension of our cognitive processes.
